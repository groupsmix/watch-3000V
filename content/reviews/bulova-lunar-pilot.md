## First Impressions

The Bulova Lunar Pilot hits you with its presence before it hits you with its story.

At 45mm with a black PVD case and a busy, purpose-driven dial, the Lunar Pilot is not subtle. Three chronograph sub-dials fill the face: a running seconds counter at 9 o'clock, a 30-minute counter at 12, and a 1/10th-second counter at 6. The tachymeter scale wraps around the bezel. The date window sits at 4 o'clock. Two chronograph pushers flank the crown. Everything about the design says "this watch was built to measure things that matter."

Then you flip it over and read the caseback: an engraving commemorating the Apollo 15 lunar mission and Commander Dave Scott. And the story begins.

In 1971, Dave Scott's government-issued Omega Speedmaster — the "Moonwatch" that NASA selected for all space missions — failed during the Apollo 15 moonwalk. Its crystal had popped off due to pressure changes. Scott switched to his personal Bulova chronograph, which he'd quietly brought along as a backup. That Bulova completed the mission on the lunar surface. In 2015, it sold at auction for $1.625 million.

This watch is the reissue. And the story — true, documented, extraordinary — is the most powerful gift-wrapping any watch has ever had. You're not just giving a chronograph. You're giving a piece of space history.

## Design & Build Quality

<!-- Image: Caseback engraving showing Apollo 15 commemorative text -->

### The Dial

The Lunar Pilot's dial is information-dense — intentionally so. The original Bulova chronograph that went to the moon was a tool watch designed for precision measurement, and this reissue faithfully recreates that utilitarian spirit. Three sub-dials (running seconds, 30-minute totalizer, 1/10th-second counter) are arranged symmetrically around the center. The main hands are sword-shaped with luminous fill. The minute track is printed in fine detail around the chapter ring.

Is it busy? Yes. Is it cluttered? No. Every element serves a function. The design rewards closer inspection — you notice new details on the third or fourth look that you missed on the first.

### The Case

At 45mm and approximately 13.5mm thick, the Lunar Pilot is the largest watch in our catalog by a notable margin. The black PVD coating gives it a tactical, purposeful look — more military tool than dressy accessory. The case shape is asymmetric: the crown and pushers are positioned at 2, 3, and 4 o'clock, with a crown guard integrated into the case on the right side. This asymmetry is faithful to the original 1971 design.

The PVD coating is durable and scratch-resistant. Over years of wear, it may show minor wear marks at high-contact points (desk diving, clasp contact), but it holds up well for a coated surface.

### The Crystal

Sapphire — flat, anti-reflective, and clear. At this price point with a chronograph complication, sapphire crystal is a significant value add. The AR coating reduces glare on the busy dial, improving legibility in bright conditions.

### The Strap

The default nylon strap with Velcro closure is a direct nod to the original NASA equipment strap — functional, lightweight, and easy to adjust on the fly. It's comfortable from day one and breathes well in warm weather. The watch is also available with a leather strap or steel bracelet (variants vary by retailer). The 20mm lug width allows easy aftermarket swaps.

### The Caseback

The engraved caseback is the Lunar Pilot's most emotionally powerful design element. The Apollo 15 commemorative text, mission dates, and Commander Scott's association are permanently etched into steel. It's the detail that transforms this from "a nice chronograph" to "a piece of history." Every time the recipient takes the watch off, they see the story.

**Build quality verdict:** The Lunar Pilot delivers sapphire crystal, high-precision quartz chronograph, and PVD coating at $375–$475. The chronograph functionality alone (1/10th-second precision) competes with Swiss mechanical chronographs at $2,000+. The heritage story is priceless.

## Movement & Accuracy

The Lunar Pilot runs on Bulova's proprietary 262 kHz high-performance quartz movement — and it's genuinely exceptional.

**Key movement specs:**

| Spec | Detail |
|------|--------|
| Type | High-Performance Quartz |
| Frequency | 262,144 Hz (262 kHz) |
| Accuracy | ±10 seconds per year |
| Chronograph | 1/10th second precision |
| Battery Life | ~3–4 years |

Standard quartz movements operate at 32,768 Hz (32 kHz). The Lunar Pilot operates at 262 kHz — eight times the frequency. This isn't marketing fluff: the higher frequency directly translates to dramatically improved accuracy. Where a standard quartz watch drifts ±15 seconds per month, the Lunar Pilot drifts ±10 seconds per year. He will set the time once and forget about it for months.

The chronograph measures to 1/10th of a second — visible on the sub-dial at 6 o'clock with a separate hand that sweeps through 10 divisions per second. For practical use (timing runs, laps, cooking, presentations), this is more precision than he'll ever need. But it's impressive to demonstrate, and it connects directly to the watch's tool-watch heritage: this is a chronograph designed to measure things that matter, with the precision to back it up.

The sweeping seconds hand moves at 16 ticks per second — smoother than any quartz watch in our catalog and approaching the visual smoothness of an automatic. It's one of the first things watch-aware people notice about the Lunar Pilot.

## Comfort & Wearability

The Lunar Pilot's size is polarizing — and honest conversation about it is important for gift-givers.

At 45mm and 13.5mm thick, this is a big watch. On wrists under 7", it will wear noticeably large. The asymmetric case and chronograph pushers add to the visual footprint. The crown guard extends the case width on the right side. This is not a watch for people who prefer their timepieces subtle.

On 7"+ wrists, it wears confidently — the thickness sits proportionally, and the black PVD coating actually helps reduce visual mass by absorbing light rather than reflecting it. The nylon strap (approximately 35g) keeps the overall weight surprisingly low, making all-day wear comfortable despite the case size.

**Dress-up potential:** Limited. The 45mm black PVD case and military-tool aesthetic are too casual and bold for suits and formal events. It can stretch to a blazer-and-jeans smart-casual look, but no further. For dressy occasions, see the [Hamilton Jazzmaster](/reviews/hamilton-jazzmaster-open-heart/) or [Tissot Gentleman](/reviews/tissot-gentleman-powermatic-80/).

**Dress-down potential:** Excellent. The Lunar Pilot was designed for the most extreme casual environment imaginable — the surface of the moon. T-shirts, hoodies, flight jackets, cargo pants — it belongs. The tactical-tool aesthetic adds character to any casual outfit.

**Daily wearability:** 3–5 days per week, lifestyle-dependent. Best for men who lean casual or have a "weekend warrior" wardrobe. The 50m water resistance handles rain and hand-washing but not swimming. The sapphire crystal handles daily bumps. The nylon strap handles sweat and heat.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Did the Bulova Lunar Pilot actually go to the moon?

Yes — the original Bulova chronograph (the predecessor to this modern reissue) was worn by astronaut Dave Scott during the Apollo 15 mission in 1971. When his government-issued Omega Speedmaster failed during a moonwalk, Scott used his personal Bulova to complete the mission. That original watch sold at auction in 2015 for $1.625 million. The modern Lunar Pilot is a faithful reissue of that watch.

### Is the Bulova Lunar Pilot a good gift?

Outstanding — it's the best "story watch" in our catalog. The Apollo 15 heritage gives the gift-giver a compelling narrative to tell when presenting the watch. Combined with the high-precision 262 kHz quartz movement, sapphire crystal, and chronograph complications, it delivers both emotional impact and technical substance. Gift-Worthiness Score: 9.2/10.

### Is the Bulova Lunar Pilot an automatic or quartz watch?

Quartz — but not standard quartz. The Lunar Pilot uses Bulova's proprietary 262 kHz high-performance quartz movement, which operates at eight times the frequency of standard quartz. This delivers ±10 seconds per year accuracy (vs. ±15 seconds per month for standard quartz) and a smooth-sweeping seconds hand that approaches automatic-like visual quality.

### How big is the Bulova Lunar Pilot?

45mm diameter and approximately 13.5mm thick — it's a large watch. We recommend it for wrists 7 inches and above for proportional wear. On smaller wrists, consider the [Hamilton Khaki Field](/reviews/hamilton-khaki-field-mechanical/) (38mm) or [Tissot PRX](/reviews/tissot-prx/) (40mm) for a similar price-range gift.

### How does the Bulova Lunar Pilot compare to the Omega Speedmaster?

Both have legitimate lunar heritage. The Omega Speedmaster "Moonwatch" ($6,000+) was NASA's official space-rated chronograph. The Bulova Lunar Pilot ($375–$475) was Dave Scott's personal backup that completed the Apollo 15 mission when the Speedmaster failed. The Speedmaster is a luxury mechanical chronograph; the Lunar Pilot is a high-precision quartz chronograph at roughly 7% of the price. Both have authentic space stories — the Bulova's is arguably more dramatic.

### Is the Bulova Lunar Pilot waterproof?

Rated at 50m (5 ATM) — suitable for rain, hand-washing, and accidental splashes. Not suitable for swimming or water sports. For water activities, see the [Seiko 5 Sports SRPD55](/reviews/seiko-5-srpd55/) (100m) or [Tissot PRX](/reviews/tissot-prx/) (100m).
